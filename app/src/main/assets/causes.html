Causes of Leukemia

Possible Causes and Risk Factors

  • Genetic mutations in blood-forming cells
  • Exposure to high levels of radiation
  • Previous cancer treatment with chemotherapy or radiation therapy
  • Exposure to certain chemicals (e.g., benzene)
  • Inherited genetic disorders (e.g., Down syndrome)
  • Smoking, which increases risk for some types of leukemia
  • Family history of leukemia

Understanding Leukemia Development

Leukemia begins when genetic changes cause bone marrow cells to grow and divide uncontrollably. Over time, these abnormal cells crowd out healthy blood cells, leading to the symptoms and complications associated with the disease.
